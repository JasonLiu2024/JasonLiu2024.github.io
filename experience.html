Meta-learning and multi-task learning for Physics Discovery

My team and I used Meta-learning and multi-task learning to empower research on the Whispering Gallery Mode phenomenon
Our goal is to use Deep Neural Networks to extract the relationship between a the behavior of a laser beam and factors like temperature and pressure.
The spectrum of a laser beam records its behavior through different medium.
Physics theory indicate that temperature and perssure impact that behavior.
However, it's difficult to discern such impact from the spectrum directly, because it has lots of noise.
More over, Whispering-Gallery-Mode experiments are expensive to conduct, and it's difficult to keep conditions like temperature stable.

  • The input is a 10,000-long spectrum from a laser beam
  • The outputs are numbers representing factors like temperature, pressure, and the intensity of that laser beam

I implemented different machine learning algorithms, trained them, and evaluated their performance by comparing results.
I did so for the following algorithms:

  • Dense Neural Network, to serve as a baseline
  • Siamese Algorithm
  • Triplet Algorithm
  • Mult-task training scheme 1: train on datasets sequentially
  • Mult-task training scheme 2: train on a super-dataset inter-woven from other datasets in order

MRI Image Reconstruction using the U-Net Algorithm

I implemented the U-Net algorithm to make a noisy MRI image into a sharper
My dataset is a sharp MRI image. I created input data by applying Fourier transform, using the original sharp image as its label
I applied data augmentation to this dataset. My methods are:

  • Perspective
  • Rotate
  • Flip
  • Crop

I evaluated the result (reconstructed image) by taking its Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR) and Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM) against the label image
