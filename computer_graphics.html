Geometry Processing for Archaeology Discovery

My team and I extracted geometric features from LiDAR point clouds and Groud-Penetrating-Radar (GPR) images
Our goal is to discover and evaluate archaeological features on the surface that aren't obvious to the human eye
Very High-Resolution (VHR) LiDAR scan from a drone extracts captures the surface of a dig site in detail
GPR scan from a trolley detects architectural remains underground
By analyzing mesh reconstructed from LiDAR point clouds, we drew 3D ridge lines on areas of high curvature.
Similarly, we extracted 2D ridge lines from the GPR image.
Correspondence between 3D and 2D ridge lines reveal architectural patterns and structural patterns not highlighted by conventional methods

1. I implemented different geometry processing algorithms, comparing their effectiveness in analyzing our 3D and 2D data.
I did so for the following algorithms:

  • CrestCODE
  • Mejering
  • 2D image ridges

2. I optimized and simplified the structure of my code, creating a simple interface
3. I participated in weekly lab meetings with my advisors, where we discuss next steps and evaluate our current progress.

Rendering the Venation of Insect Wings using Computer Vision techniques

I implemented the U-Net algorithm to make a noisy MRI image into a sharper
My dataset is a sharp MRI image. I created input data by applying Fourier transform, using the original sharp image as its label
I applied data augmentation to this dataset. My methods are:

  • Perspective
  • Rotate
  • Flip
  • Crop

I evaluated the result (reconstructed image) by taking its Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR) and Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM) against the label image
