Art and Design

3D modeling (CAD and mesh) and scanning, sketching, and game design

3D modeling and scanning

I am an expert in SolidWorks surfacing and mesh modeling. And I'm an intermediate in computational design with Grasshopper (a part of Rhino3D).
I have been an avid modeler since senior year in high school. I am particularly interested in capturing 3D forms in a precise and simple way.
I began by modeling in Blender, a mesh-modeling software–I still use it in my computer graphics research! After developing and expertise in mesh (re)topology and modeling, realized the limitations of mesh modeling–its lack of precision and editability.
This brought me to SolidWorks, from which I learnt about Differential Geometry and Computer Graphics. Still, I'm unsatisfied by the explicit and sometimes tedious nature of manual CAD modeling, despite it's power
That led me to computational design, where I use coding to empower, implement, and generate designs
My favorite computational design projects are:

  • Visualization of the Quaternion Juliet Set
  • A Circular Maze generator
  • A pebble pattern generator for organic NURBs surfaces

    Pixel Biker (and more coming up!)

    I took Video Game Programming I and am currently taking II, its continuation to prepare myself for the rapidly growing industry of video game development, which increasingly draw on techniques from computer graphics and machine learning (computer vision and natural language processing)
    I led a team of 3 to build Pixel Biker, a 2D physics game where the player character surmounts obstacles by building and applying momentum at the right time
    We used Unity and C# and built this game in 2 months. I developed the sound effects, the UI, the terrain, and the many pickup items. I also conducted many player-testing sessions to iron out the value of important parameters like gravity and friction.
